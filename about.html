About

I provide marine electrical troubleshooting and systems diagnostics for sailboats and powerboats in the San Francisco Bay Area. My focus is on solving real electrical problems methodically — not guessing, not swapping parts, and not leaving systems in worse shape than I found them.

Many boats suffer from years of incremental upgrades, undocumented wiring changes, corrosion, and poorly supported connections. My goal is to restore clarity and reliability to those systems with clean, deliberate work.

Approach

Electrical issues on boats are rarely random. They follow patterns: voltage drop, poor terminations, grounding faults, interference, moisture intrusion, or improper circuit protection.

I approach each issue with a structured diagnostic process:

  • Verify the symptom
  • Measure and isolate the circuit
  • Identify root cause
  • Correct cleanly and safely
  • Leave documentation and labeling where appropriate

The goal is not just to make something work — it’s to make it understandable and reliable going forward.

Background & Experience

My professional background is in electrical systems and technical field work. I bring an engineering mindset to marine troubleshooting, with an emphasis on safety, structured testing, and long-term reliability.

I have experience working with:

  • Alternators and external regulators
  • Battery banks (AGM, flooded, LiFePO₄ planning)
  • Shore power systems and AC safety considerations
  • NMEA 2000 networks and device troubleshooting
  • Bilge pump circuits and critical safety systems
  • Corrosion diagnosis and termination upgrades

While not operating as a full-service boatyard, I maintain a strong awareness of ABYC principles and marine electrical best practices. Work is performed with attention to proper circuit protection, strain relief, corrosion resistance, and clear routing.

Personal Standard

I own and maintain my own sailboat and apply the same standards to client vessels that I expect on my own. Clean terminations, labeled circuits, supported wiring, and thoughtful routing are not optional details — they are baseline requirements.

Boats operate in a harsh environment. Vibration, salt air, and moisture expose shortcuts quickly. My work prioritizes durability and clarity over speed.
