Background

I bring experience from mission-critical electrical environments — including U.S. Navy and Coast Guard settings — followed by complex energy systems commissioning and field diagnostics in the private sector. In those environments, electrical failures are not inconveniences. They are operational risks. That mindset shapes how I approach marine systems today.

Before focusing on boats, my work centered on high-reliability infrastructure such as:

  • AC and DC power distribution
  • Battery systems and backup power
  • Motor controls and critical loads
  • Ground fault detection and fault isolation
  • Power quality troubleshooting
  • Monitoring, alarms, and controls
  • Structured preventative maintenance programs

Why marine

Boats combine everything that makes electrical work hard: vibration, moisture, corrosion, limited access, mixed AC/DC systems, and years of add-on electronics layered over time.

Most persistent issues aren’t caused by a single bad part — they come from incomplete installations, undocumented modifications, or small compromises that stack up. My job is to restore clarity and confidence to your system.

What clients can expect

  • Methodical troubleshooting with measured data
  • Clean, ABYC-aligned workmanship
  • Clear documentation of what was tested and what was found
  • Practical recommendations, not upsells
  • Long-term system thinking (stability first, then simplicity, then performance)

The name “Sure Shot”

In complex systems, guessing creates failures. Sure Shot represents precision, accountability, and doing the job correctly the first time — so when you leave the dock, your electrical system is not something you worry about.
