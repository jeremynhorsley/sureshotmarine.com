About Sure Shot Marine Systems

Electrical systems built for reliability, not guesswork.

Sure Shot Marine Systems was built on one principle: electrical systems should be predictable, documented, and dependable.

I bring over three decades of experience working in mission-critical electrical environments — including service in both U.S. Navy and Coast Guard settings, followed by utility-scale energy system commissioning and troubleshooting in the private sector.

In those environments, electrical failures are not inconveniences. They are operational risks. That mindset shapes everything I do today.

Background

Before focusing on marine systems, I supported high-reliability power infrastructure including:

  • AC and DC power distribution systems
  • Battery backup and energy storage systems
  • Motor control and propulsion support systems
  • Ground fault detection and fault isolation
  • Power quality analysis and harmonics troubleshooting
  • Alarm, monitoring, and controls systems
  • Structured preventative maintenance programs

I later transitioned into large-scale energy commissioning, developing procedures, troubleshooting complex system faults, and supporting live operational deployments.

What that means for marine clients is simple: I approach your vessel the same way critical infrastructure is approached — with discipline, documentation, and a focus on long-term reliability.

Why Marine?

Boats combine everything that makes electrical work challenging:

  • Vibration
  • Moisture
  • Corrosion
  • Limited access
  • Mixed AC/DC systems
  • Add-on electronics layered over decades

Most marine electrical issues are not caused by a single failed component — they are caused by incomplete installations, undocumented modifications, or cumulative small compromises.

My goal is not just to fix the symptom. My goal is to restore clarity and confidence to your system.

What Clients Can Expect

  • Methodical troubleshooting
  • Clean, ABYC-aligned workmanship
  • Clear documentation of work performed
  • Practical recommendations, not upsells
  • Long-term system thinking

Whether it’s battery systems, charging architecture, power quality concerns, NMEA networks, or full system reviews — the approach remains the same:

Stability first. Simplicity second. Performance third.

The Name “Sure Shot”

In complex systems, guessing creates failures.

Sure Shot represents precision, accountability, and doing the job correctly the first time. Because when you leave the dock, your electrical system should not be something you worry about.
