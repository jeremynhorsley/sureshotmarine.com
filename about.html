Background

My background is in electrical engineering and high-reliability power systems. Before focusing on boats, I worked in U.S. Navy and Coast Guard environments and later in the private sector commissioning and troubleshooting complex energy infrastructure.

In those environments, troubleshooting has to be methodical and accountable. Decisions are based on measured data, verified operation, and clear documentation — not assumption and parts-swapping. That mindset translates well to marine systems.

Marine systems

Boats combine conditions that make electrical work uniquely challenging: vibration, moisture, corrosion, limited access, mixed AC/DC architecture, and years of incremental modifications layered on top of each other.

Many persistent issues aren’t caused by a single failed component. They come from compounding small problems: incomplete installations, undocumented changes, undersized conductors, poor terminations, or protection schemes that don’t match how the boat is actually used.

The goal is clarity — so you understand what you have, what is working, what isn’t, and what matters most to fix.

How I work

  • Measured diagnostics before recommendations
  • Protection, grounding, and bonding verified where relevant
  • Clear explanation of findings in plain language
  • Documented notes and next steps when appropriate
  • Practical priorities based on your goals (weekends, cruising, liveaboard)

What this service is (and isn’t)

Sure Shot Marine is focused on electrical diagnostics, charging systems, onboard networks, and system-level improvements such as solar integration and electrical system mapping.

If a project requires specialized mechanical, fiberglass, rigging, or engine work, I’ll recommend the right professional or coordinate around that scope so the electrical side is done correctly and safely.
