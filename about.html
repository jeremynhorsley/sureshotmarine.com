About

Marine electrical specialist serving Bay Area sailboats and powerboats since 2012, with a background in U.S. Coast Guard and Navy electronics.

My name is Jeremyn Horsley. I've been troubleshooting electrical and electronic systems in marine environments since 1995, and have been providing marine electrical services in the Bay Area since 2012.

I started out boating just like any other kid in the 80's living in a trailer park near a lake with an old rowboat. One thing led to another, and then to the Navy, and long story short... I now currently sail a 1990 Ericson 32-200.

Background

B.S. in Electrical Engineering. U.S. Navy avionics aboard the USS Enterprise, then several years as a Coast Guard electronics technician — maintaining 47' motor lifeboats, serving aboard a cutter, and supporting Rescue21 and station boat electronics across the district. After that, civilian work troubleshooting industrial power systems before focusing on marine.

Technical Scope

  • DC and AC fault isolation
  • Charging and battery system diagnostics
  • NMEA 2000 network troubleshooting
  • Shore power and distribution evaluation (scope-dependent)
  • Bilge and critical circuit verification
  • System mapping and documentation

Method

  • Verify reported condition
  • Measure and isolate affected circuit
  • Identify root cause
  • Correct using appropriate protection and routing
  • Document findings where applicable

Service Area

Based in the East Bay, serving Alameda, Oakland, Berkeley, Richmond, Sausalito, and surrounding Bay Area marinas.
