Finding Gremlins

Most electrical problems on boats aren't obvious. They're hidden in connectors, buried in bilges, and invisible until something stops working at the wrong moment.

These are two examples from recent jobs. A failed solder joint causing an intermittent power fault, and a corroded intermittently working circuit inside a supposedly watertight connector.

What's that smell?

A factory wire tie was never removed from this VHF during installation. At some point the steel core inside the plastic sleeve made contact with 12VDC.

The current through the wire tie is likely what generated enough heat to melt not only its own plastic cover, but the insulation to the negative VHF wire, leaving bare copper exposed.

Luckily, this wasn't a permanent short, and only melted the insulation of a short section of wire. It could have been much worse.
