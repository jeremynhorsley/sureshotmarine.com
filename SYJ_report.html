Design assumptions
1 — Solar basis
Optimistic design. Annual average peak sun hours of 5.5 hr/day used (NREL data, SF Bay). December–January Bay fog can reduce effective yield to 3.0–3.5 hr/day, increasing generator dependence during those months. Foldable panels allow manual angle optimization, partially offsetting low fixed-tilt yield assumptions.
2 — System efficiency
85% overall efficiency applied (MPPT conversion + wiring losses + battery round-trip). Conservative relative to EcoFlow published MPPT efficiency of 93%+.
3 — Starlink power draw
150W assumed (high-performance model, always-on streaming). Actual draw should be confirmed with a clamp meter at installation. Standard Gen 3 Starlink (~100W) would reduce total load to ~278W and improve all sizing margins accordingly.
4 — Battery depth of discharge
90% DoD applied to rated 4,096 Wh capacity, yielding 3,686 Wh usable. LFP chemistry supports this cycle depth without accelerated degradation at moderate ambient temperatures.
5 — Generator sizing
Honda EU2200i matched at 1,800W to EcoFlow DELTA Pro 3 AC charge rate of 1,800W — exact match. Load factor ~82% of rated maximum, within Honda's continuous duty rating. Fuel estimate based on 0.3–0.4 gal/hr at half load.
Open items prior to procurement
⚠ Gateway Max PSU oversubscription
The UniFi Cloud Gateway Max has an internal 100W PSU. Camera PoE demand is approximately 118.5W (10 cameras × average draw), which exceeds PSU capacity before accounting for chassis overhead (~40W). A dedicated PoE switch (e.g. UniFi Switch Flex Mini, ~$49) is recommended to offload camera power. This issue is independent of the power system and must be resolved to achieve 100% uptime.
⚠ Starlink model confirmation
Actual Starlink model and power brick wattage should be confirmed on-site with a clamp meter before finalizing battery sizing. A 50W variance in Starlink draw meaningfully affects the daily energy balance and generator runtime calculations. Use the calculator above to model both scenarios.
⚠ USCG fuel storage compliance
Generator fuel must be stored in a USCG-approved portable container, topside, in a ventilated location. Generator must operate on open deck only — never below or in an enclosed space. CO-Minder provides automatic shutoff but does not substitute for proper placement and ventilation.
